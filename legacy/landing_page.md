## MISSION SECTION

**Title:** Why This Matters Now

The Holocaust survivors who lived through the darkest chapter in human history are in their final years. Their stories — of courage, faith, resistance, and survival — are irreplaceable.

At the same time, antisemitism is rising to its highest recorded level since World War II. A new generation of students is encountering this hatred without the historical grounding to understand it or stand against it.

**The Legacy Project exists to close both gaps.**

Through documentary storytelling, educational content, and public awareness media, we preserve firsthand testimony and bring it into classrooms, communities, and screens worldwide — ensuring these stories are never forgotten.

---

## WHAT WE DO

**Documentary Films**  
We produce professional documentary films featuring survivor testimony and intergenerational Jewish stories, designed for festival distribution, streaming, and educational licensing.

**Educational Content**  
Our curriculum materials are designed for university and K-12 integration — bringing Holocaust history and antisemitism education into the classroom with rigor and humanity.

**Public Awareness Campaigns**  
We create digital media campaigns that reach Jewish and non-Jewish audiences on social platforms, news media, and community channels.

**Campus Programs**  
In partnership with Hillel chapters and Jewish student organizations, we bring our content to university campuses — where antisemitism is most visible and most urgent.
